Director's Statement

We asked three young filmmakers a simple question: can you recall an unplanned moment that changed everything? What followed was never scripted.

The Unplanned Deed is, in itself, an unplanned deed. What began as a brainstorming session became something none of us anticipated — an experimental documentary where three people sit with a camera and try to trace the invisible threads between who they were as children and who they are becoming now.

Each story in this film started with a moment nobody planned. A small gesture, an accident of timing, a presence that didn't know it mattered. Yet these moments shaped everything — quietly, irreversibly, the way water shapes stone without ever meaning to.

We didn't set out to make a film about the butterfly effect. We set out to remember. But memory, it turns out, has a way of revealing the weight of things we thought were weightless. The conversations unraveled something real — proof that the smallest, most ordinary moments carry consequences none of us could have predicted.

At the end, we return to the beginning: childhood footage none of us had watched in years. The proof that it all happened. That the small deeds were real. That they still are.
